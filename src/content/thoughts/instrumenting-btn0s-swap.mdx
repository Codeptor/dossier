I wanted the dossier-stack swap on this site to feel exactly like btn0s.dev's. Eyeballing curves never works — they look right at one duration and wrong at the next. So I instrumented.

In Playwright, I dispatched a synthetic pointer drag on the active sheet (200px right over ~250ms) and ran a `requestAnimationFrame` loop sampling `getComputedStyle(el).transform` every frame. Re-querying each frame matters because the DOM is replaced when the route swaps. After ~199 samples spanning a full navigation, three things were obvious:

1. **During drag the active sheet only translates X.** Y stayed at 0 throughout, rotation stayed at 0, scale stayed at 1. No follow-the-finger tilt — just a flat horizontal slide.
2. **The release-to-rest morph runs ~450ms** with no overshoot. I fit the value series and the closest cubic-bezier was `cubic-bezier(0.32, 0, 0, 1)` — emphasized ease-out, asymptotic settle.
3. **Resting positions are pixel-exact constants.** Left side `(-55.286, 16.7369) rotate(-4.89055deg) scale(0.98)`. Right side `(38.7471, 9.71402) rotate(3.14135deg) scale(0.99)`. No rounding, those are the literal values their motion library is targeting.

Behind the scenes that site uses Framer Motion springs. We can't reproduce a spring with a fixed duration curve, but we can ride the line within a couple of pixels by matching the duration and dropping a bezier that mimics the deceleration shape.

Worth doing once. Now I have a rule: before tuning an animation by feel, instrument the reference. RAF + getBoundingClientRect costs nothing and removes ten rounds of "is this faster or slower than theirs."
